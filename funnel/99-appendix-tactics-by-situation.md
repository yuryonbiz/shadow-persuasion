# Appendix

## Tactics By Situation

If you picked up this book because of a specific situation, use this appendix first. Find the situation closest to yours below. Read the tactics listed for it in the order given. Come back to the rest of the book later.

If you have read the full book and are now dealing with a specific conversation, use this appendix as a quick-reference. The tactics most relevant to each domain are grouped here with the chapter they appear in.

### Career And Work

For negotiating a raise, landing a promotion, handling office politics, being taken seriously at work.

- **Tactic #4: The Assumption Audit** (Ch 3). Before you walk into a review or conversation you have been bracing for, check what the meeting is actually about.
- **Tactic #7: The False Concession** (Ch 4). When pushing back is making things worse, agree with the weakest version of what they are saying.
- **Tactic #11: The Strategic Pause** (Ch 6). Four seconds of silence after their offer. They revise it themselves.
- **Tactic #17: The Earlier Agreement** (Ch 8). Reference something they said or committed to earlier. Consistency does the work.
- **Tactic #25: The Stakes Shift** (Ch 10). When the conversation is stuck on money, reframe what is actually being negotiated.
- **Tactic #28: The Third Option** (Ch 10). When given a binary, propose the alternative that solves the underlying problem.
- **Tactic #40: The Public Witness** (Ch 14). Transfer private commitments into shared awareness without enforcement.
- **Tactic #44: The Post-Agreement Bridge** (Ch 15). In the days between a verbal commitment and formal paperwork, send one unrelated warm note.

### Business And Sales

For closing clients, negotiating contracts, investor pitches, getting replies on cold outreach.

- **Tactic #1: The Cold Open** (Ch 3). Open with a specific observation about their situation. No preamble.
- **Tactic #2: The Pre-Frame** (Ch 3). A twenty-word email before the call changes how the call lands.
- **Tactic #6: The Confession First** (Ch 4). Name what is wrong with your proposal before they find it.
- **Tactic #8: The Unrepayable Favor** (Ch 5). A small, specific gift with no follow-up ask.
- **Tactic #11: The Strategic Pause** (Ch 6). The silence does the negotiating.
- **Tactic #19: The Pre-Understood Objection** (Ch 8). State their real concern before they do.
- **Tactic #21: The Discovery Path** (Ch 9). Four questions that lead to the conclusion you want them to reach.
- **Tactic #27: The Timeline Compression** (Ch 10). Collapse the decision window explicitly.
- **Tactic #31: The Reverse Qualifier** (Ch 12). Qualify yourself out and watch them pursue.
- **Tactic #37: The Unclosing** (Ch 13). When they agree, do not celebrate. Move on.
- **Tactic #41: The One-Line Email** (Ch 14). Twelve words. No summary. No terms.
- **Tactic #45: The Escalation Ladder** (Ch 15). Pre-planned rungs across months or years.

### Money And Personal Finance

For negotiating purchases, dealing with contractors, refunds, exceptions from policy, advocating with doctors.

- **Tactic #7: The False Concession** (Ch 4). Concede the surface argument. The real issue surfaces.
- **Tactic #11: The Strategic Pause** (Ch 6). Four seconds of silence after their number.
- **Tactic #24: The Reverse Priority** (Ch 9). Ask them to rank their concerns. Address the last one.
- **Tactic #27: The Timeline Compression** (Ch 10). Collapse the back-and-forth into a single window.
- **Tactic #28: The Third Option** (Ch 10). A genuinely different proposal that solves both sides.
- **Tactic #30: The Hidden Cost** (Ch 11). Surface a cost they have not priced in.
- **Tactic #41: The One-Line Email** (Ch 14). Short written follow-up after verbal agreement.
- **Tactic #47: The Last-Word Exit** (Ch 15). Calibrate their emotional state for the next touch.

### Leadership And Managing People

For getting buy-in from your team, handling underperformers, making hard calls, earning respect.

- **Tactic #5: The Consultant Stance** (Ch 4). Diagnose before prescribing.
- **Tactic #10: The Reflective Echo** (Ch 5). Echo their concern at higher precision than they asked it.
- **Tactic #14: The Borrowed Conclusion** (Ch 7). A story about a third party reaching the conclusion you want them to reach.
- **Tactic #22: The Misdirected Question** (Ch 9). A peripheral question that reveals the underlying position.
- **Tactic #25: The Stakes Shift** (Ch 10). Reframe what is actually at risk.
- **Tactic #34: The Mutual Enemy** (Ch 12). Align against the external constraint, not against each other.
- **Tactic #40: The Public Witness** (Ch 14). Private commitments become shared awareness.
- **Tactic #45: The Escalation Ladder** (Ch 15). Long-term trust and buy-in are built across small steps.

### Dating And Attraction

For first dates, getting an ex back, handling distance, defining the relationship.

- **Tactic #1: The Cold Open** (Ch 3). Specific observations beat smooth openers.
- **Tactic #3: The 3-Second Read** (Ch 3). Read them before you say anything.
- **Tactic #9: The Mirror They Don't Catch** (Ch 5). Adopt their distinctive phrases with a lag.
- **Tactic #16: The Preview** (Ch 7). Describe a future moment in specific detail.
- **Tactic #23: The Two Doors** (Ch 9). Offer two options, not one proposal.
- **Tactic #26: The Cinderella Frame** (Ch 9). Offer and pull back slightly.
- **Tactic #31: The Reverse Qualifier** (Ch 12). Disqualify yourself genuinely.
- **Tactic #36: The Disqualification Move** (Ch 12). Remove a specific topic from the conversation.
- **Tactic #38: The Future Echo** (Ch 13). Install the post-decision reality.
- **Tactic #46: The Permanent Status** (Ch 15). Language that treats the relationship as established.

### Relationships And Social

For family, friendships, boundaries, toxic people, making a first impression.

- **Tactic #1: The Cold Open** (Ch 3). Works equally well with estranged family members.
- **Tactic #10: The Reflective Echo** (Ch 5). Rare in families. Transformative when used.
- **Tactic #11: The Strategic Pause** (Ch 6). Silence breaks more patterns than arguing.
- **Tactic #12: The Reverse Priority** (Ch 6). The real fight is never the surface fight.
- **Tactic #14: The Borrowed Conclusion** (Ch 7). Stories work where direct arguments have failed.
- **Tactic #18: The Named Feeling** (Ch 8). Offer a guess about their underlying emotion. Let them correct.
- **Tactic #20: The Small Yes That Isn't** (Ch 8). Establish an adjacent principle before the real ask.
- **Tactic #26: The Category Move** (Ch 10). "We are doing the thing again. Let's not."
- **Tactic #32: The Misclassification** (Ch 11). Correct a wrong label matter-of-factly.
- **Tactic #41: The Waiting Period** (Ch 13). Let a yes solidify before pushing to action.
- **Tactic #47: The Last-Word Exit** (Ch 15). The final sentence is what they remember.

### Parenting

For teenagers, co-parenting, boundaries, hard conversations with kids.

- **Tactic #6: The Confession First** (Ch 4). Kids can smell performance from across the room.
- **Tactic #7: The False Concession** (Ch 4). Agree with their complaint. The real issue surfaces.
- **Tactic #11: The Strategic Pause** (Ch 6). Silence gets teenagers to volunteer what direct questions cannot.
- **Tactic #15: The Third Party Story** (Ch 7). A story from your own teenage years, told without pulling the parallel.
- **Tactic #18: The Named Feeling** (Ch 8). Offered as a guess, never as a diagnosis.
- **Tactic #23: The Two Doors** (Ch 9). Present two options, not an ultimatum.
- **Tactic #35: The Volcano** (Ch 12). Used carefully. Most teenage escalations are escalation patterns.

### Defend And Protect

For manipulators, liars, gaslighting, narcissists, boundary violations.

- **Tactic #5: The Consultant Stance** (Ch 4). Diagnostic questions have no counter-move in the manipulator's playbook.
- **Tactic #7: The False Concession** (Ch 4). Agreeing where they expected resistance collapses their script.
- **Tactic #18: The Named Feeling** (Ch 8). Naming the possible underlying emotion surfaces the pattern.
- **Tactic #22: The Misdirected Question** (Ch 9). The honest answer reveals what the defensive answer would have hidden.
- **Tactic #29: The Exposed Assumption** (Ch 11). Naming the assumption neutralizes the pattern.
- **Tactic #33: The Paradox Reveal** (Ch 11). Two contradictory claims surfaced neutrally.
- **Tactic #35: The Volcano** (Ch 12). Let the escalation burn out. Speak calmly when it is over.
- **Tactic #41: The Waiting Period** (Ch 13). Time is often on your side, not theirs.
